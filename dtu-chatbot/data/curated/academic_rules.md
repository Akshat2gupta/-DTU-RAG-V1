# DTU Academic Rules — Grading, Attendance, Backlogs, Exams (curated)

Hand-verified from the DTU B.Tech ordinances (2018-2025, dtu.ac.in/Web/Academics/ordinance/),
primarily the 2022/2023 ordinance text (regulations R. 1(B).3 through R. 1(B).29),
the official CGPA-conversion notice, and the Examination Branch rules.

## B.Tech course structure and first-year syllabus at DTU

Source: https://dtu.ac.in/Web/Academics/ordinance/BTech_2022_ordinance.pdf

What is the syllabus / course structure for first year B.Tech at DTU?
All branches share a common first year (Table 3 of the ordinance):

First Semester (20 credits): Mathematics-I (4), Physics-I (4), Chemistry OR
Basic Electrical Engineering (4), Basic Mechanical Engineering OR Programming
Fundamentals (4), Workshop Practice OR Engineering Graphics (2), and a
Foundation Elective (2). Second Semester (20 credits): Mathematics-II,
Physics-II and the swapped halves of the same pairs (students who did
Chemistry do Basic Electrical Engineering, etc.), plus a Foundation Elective.

From the second year, the structure is department-specific:
- 3rd/4th semester (22 credits each): Allied Engineering Courses, Department
  Core Courses (DCC), Engineering Analysis and Design, Foundation Electives.
- 5th/6th semester (~23 credits each): Department Core + Department/General
  Elective Courses (DEC/GEC) + Fundamentals of Management (HMC).
- 4th year: remaining cores/electives plus the major project (B.Tech
  project).
Course categories: ASC (Applied Science), AEC (Allied Engineering),
DCC (Department Core), DEC/GEC/OEC (Department/General/Open Electives),
FEC (Foundation Elective), HMC (Humanities & Management). The detailed
subject-wise syllabus for each branch is in the ordinance for that batch year
(dtu.ac.in/Web/Academics/ordinance/).

## Course registration, credit limits and add/drop at DTU

Source: https://dtu.ac.in/Web/Academics/ordinance/BTech_2022_ordinance.pdf

- Every student must register each semester on the scheduled date (academic
  calendar). Late registration is allowed up to 10 working days with a late
  fee (no late fee if proceeding on industrial training/internship).
- Students must FIRST register for courses they failed in previous
  years/semesters (backlogs), then the remaining courses of the semester.
- Credit limits per semester (Table 3): B.Tech — minimum 16, maximum 32
  credits; M.Tech — minimum 12, maximum 24 credits. A student blocked from
  current-semester courses due to backlogs may register below the minimum.
- Courses can be added or dropped during the first ten days of the semester.
- Credit counting: one lecture or tutorial hour per week = 1 credit; one
  laboratory/design-studio hour per week = 0.5 credit.
- An elective course runs only if at least 20 students register for it.

## Promotion, detention and termination rules at DTU

Source: https://dtu.ac.in/Web/Academics/ordinance/BTech_2022_ordinance.pdf

- A student CANNOT register for third-year courses without having cleared all
  first-year courses, and cannot register for fourth-year courses without
  having cleared all second-year courses.
- Enrolment is TERMINATED if a student fails to earn 30% of the prescribed
  first-year credits by the end of the first year.
- A first-year student continuously absent for more than four weeks without
  informing the course coordinators is removed from the university rolls and
  becomes ineligible for re-admission.
- Enrolment is cancelled on completion of the maximum programme duration. A
  terminated student may appeal to the Vice Chancellor within fifteen days;
  if allowed, enrolment is restored.

## Withdrawal from a course or semester at DTU

Source: https://dtu.ac.in/Web/Academics/ordinance/BTech_2022_ordinance.pdf

- Course withdrawal: apply through the HOD to the COE within one week from
  the end of the Mid-Term Examination (on the Program Advisor's advice). A
  withdrawal grade is recorded for the course.
- Semester withdrawal: if unable to attend classes for more than four weeks
  in a semester (e.g. health reasons), a student may apply for withdrawal
  from the whole semester — all registered courses — before the End-Term
  Examination starts. Partial semester withdrawal is not allowed.

## Make-up examinations at DTU (medical / extraordinary grounds)

Source: https://dtu.ac.in/Web/Academics/ordinance/BTech_2022_ordinance.pdf

- Students who miss the Mid-Term and/or End-Term Examination due to medical
  reasons or special circumstances may be allowed a Make-up Examination on
  the HOD's recommendation.
- Apply to the HOD within ten working days of the missed examination; late
  applications are not entertained. Approval flows HOD → COE → Dean (UG/PG).
- End-Term make-up is allowed only if the student was not disqualified for
  attendance shortage. There is NO make-up of a make-up examination.

## Unfair means in DTU examinations

Source: https://dtu.ac.in/Web/Academics/ordinance/BTech_2022_ordinance.pdf

- Adopting (or being suspected of adopting) unfair means before, during or
  after an examination — including plagiarism (copying others' work into
  class submissions, projects or dissertations without acknowledgement) —
  attracts penal action per the ordinance's unfair-means annexure.
- All cases go to the Unfair Means Scrutiny Committee: Dean (UG) chairs,
  with Dean (PG), DSW, the concerned HOD, Associate Deans (Academic) and the
  COE as convener.
- A student punished for unfair means (or serious indiscipline) loses
  Merit-cum-Means and trust scholarships, medals, prizes and awards for that
  academic session.

## Scholarships at DTU (ordinance provisions)

Source: https://dtu.ac.in/Web/Academics/ordinance/BTech_2022_ordinance.pdf

- The university awards Merit-cum-Means (MCM) scholarships, University free
  studentships, SC/ST-category university scholarships and other scholarships
  / prizes approved by the Academic Council, plus scholarships funded by
  external trusts, organizations and governments.
- DTU also grants up to 100% tuition-fee waiver to economically weaker
  students and a Rs. 10,000 merit scholarship to each B.Tech branch topper.
- External scholarships may be drawn only with the Dean's permission.

## Semester-away / student exchange programme at DTU

Source: https://dtu.ac.in/Web/Academics/ordinance/BTech_2022_ordinance.pdf

- A student may do one semester of course/project work at a university ranked
  in the top 500 of the Times Higher Education or QS rankings (last five
  years), or at a university with a DTU MoU.
- Eligibility (4-year UG): completed 4 semesters at DTU, CGPA ≥ 7.0 with NO
  backlog, and no disciplinary action. (2-year PG: 2 semesters, same CGPA
  condition.)
- 8-20 equivalent credits may be registered, against elective (DEC/GEC/OEC)
  quota only — all core courses must be completed at DTU. DTU provides no
  financial support for the programme.

## How DTU exams are conducted (MTE / ETE)

Source: https://dtu.ac.in/Web/Academics/ordinance/BTech_2022_ordinance.pdf

- Evaluation components: continuous evaluation (CWS — class work sessional,
  PRS — practical sessional, quizzes/assignments), the Mid-Term Examination
  (MTE) and the End-Term Examination (ETE/PRE), with weights per the Scheme
  of Teaching and Examination (announced by the course coordinator in the
  first week of classes).
- The MTE is conducted by the course coordinator; the ETE is conducted by
  the Controller of Examinations. MTE papers have no alternatives; ETE papers
  may have 20-30% alternative questions.
- Students can see their MTE and ETE answer sheets on a notified day and
  raise discrepancies the same day only. ETE sheets are not shown after the
  Grade Moderation Committee finalizes grades. Answer sheets are preserved
  for six months.
- Evaluation must conclude within one week (MTE) / two weeks (ETE) of the
  exam. Answer sheets cannot be written or evaluated in pencil.

## DTU grading system — grade letters, grade points and cutoffs

Source: https://dtu.ac.in/Web/Academics/ordinance/BTech_2022_ordinance.pdf

Academic performance is graded on a 10-point scale. Marks out of 100 are
converted to grades using relative grading: the mean (x̄) and standard
deviation (σ) of the class marks set the cutoffs, with absolute caps
(Table 4 of the ordinance):

| Letter grade | Meaning | Grade point | Cutoff rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| O | Outstanding | 10 | m ≥ x̄ + 1.5σ, or 91% — whichever is lower |
| A+ | Excellent | 9 | x̄ + 1.0σ ≤ m < x̄ + 1.5σ, cap 82% |
| A | Very Good | 8 | x̄ + 0.5σ ≤ m < x̄ + 1.0σ, cap 73% |
| B+ | Good | 7 | x̄ + 0.0σ ≤ m < x̄ + 0.5σ, cap 64% |
| B | Above Average | 6 | x̄ − 0.5σ ≤ m < x̄ + 0.0σ, cap 55% |
| C | Average | 5 | x̄ − 1.0σ ≤ m < x̄ − 0.5σ, cap 46% |
| P | Pass | 4 | 35 ≤ m < x̄ − 1.0σ (lower cutoff 35%) |
| F | Fail | 0 | m < 35% |

So the minimum passing marks in a course are 35%, and the minimum passing
grade is P (grade point 4). In courses with up to 30 students, grades are
prepared on absolute performance in the percentage scale instead of relative
grading. To get a passing grade a student must appear in the End-Term
Examination.

## SGPA, CGPA and converting CGPA to percentage at DTU

Source: http://dtu.ac.in/Web/notice/2018/sep/file0906.pdf

- SGPA (Semester Grade Point Average) is the credit-weighted average of grade
  points earned in a semester; CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is the
  credit-weighted average over all semesters so far.
- Official conversion (DTU notice DTU/105(604)Acad-UG/2018-19, Sept 2018):
  Percentage = CGPA x 10. Applicable to B.Tech (and B.Tech Evening) from the
  2015-16 batch onwards and to all other programmes from the 2017-18 batch
  onwards.
- For employment or external requirements, a CGPA of 6.0 or above is to be
  treated as First Division.
- An 'F' grade is not counted in the CGPA but is counted in the SGPA, and it
  stays permanently on the grade card.

## DTU attendance rules

Source: https://dtu.ac.in/Web/Academics/ordinance/BTech_2022_ordinance.pdf

- Minimum attendance of 75% of all classes (lectures, tutorials, practicals)
  held in a subject is required to be eligible to appear in the End-Term
  Examination of that subject.
- The Dean (UG)/Dean (PG), authorized by the Vice Chancellor, may relax the
  minimum attendance by up to 10% for recorded reasons (e.g. documented
  participation in university activities or medical grounds).
- A student detained for shortage of attendance gets an 'F' grade in that
  course and may clear it through the summer semester.

## What happens if I get a backlog (F grade) at DTU

Source: https://dtu.ac.in/Web/Academics/ordinance/BTech_2023_ordinance.pdf

A "backlog" at DTU means an 'F' grade in a course. Per the B.Tech ordinances:

- The 'F' grade denotes poor performance (failing the course). An 'F' grade is
  also awarded for poor attendance (below the required 75%) and can result
  from involvement in unfair means.
- An 'F' grade secured in any course stays permanently on the grade card.
  Its weight is not counted in the calculation of the CGPA, but it is counted
  in the calculation of the SGPA.
- If the 'F' grade is in a Core paper, the student has to repeat the course
  either in the summer semester or in a subsequent semester when it is
  offered.
- If the 'F' grade is in an Elective paper, the student may take the same
  course or any other course from the same category in the subsequent
  semester.
- If a student fails the major/minor project, he/she has to repeat it as a
  new project, working full time on it for a minimum period of four months.
- A student is not detained merely for having backlogs in individual courses,
  but year-to-year promotion requires earning the minimum credits prescribed
  in the ordinance for that batch; failing to earn them can stop promotion to
  the next year.
- The B.Tech degree must be completed within the maximum duration (normal
  duration 4 years; maximum 7 years counted from initial registration,
  including periods of withdrawal or absence). Enrolment stands cancelled on
  completion of the maximum duration; a terminated student may appeal to the
  Vice Chancellor within fifteen days.

## Summer semester at DTU — clearing backlogs

Source: https://dtu.ac.in/Web/Academics/ordinance/BTech_2023_ordinance.pdf

- Students having an 'F' grade in a subject in either or both the odd and
  even semesters of the current academic year are allowed to register for the
  summer semester. This also applies to students detained for shortage of
  attendance and students whose results were withheld for unfair means.
- Only common courses (first year), departmental core courses (DCC) and
  allied engineering courses (AEC) are offered in the summer semester.
- The summer semester duration is 45 days, with the same contact hours (LTP)
  as prescribed in the syllabus. Regular classroom teaching is conducted for
  courses where at least 5% of the approved intake registers.
- The summer semester fee is Rs. 5,000 + Rs. 500 per course; registration is
  allowed only after depositing the fee.
- Summer semester registration is offered from the AY 2020-21 batches
  onwards, and the earlier provision of supplementary examinations was
  dropped at the same time.

## I grade — missing the end-term exam (medical or special circumstances)

Source: https://dtu.ac.in/Web/Academics/ordinance/BTech_2022_ordinance.pdf

- If a student is absent from the End-Term Examination of a course due to
  medical reasons or other special circumstances, he/she may apply for the
  award of an 'I' (Incomplete) grade to the Controller of Examinations
  through the Course Coordinator — provided the student was not disqualified
  for shortage of attendance.
- The course coordinator must be convinced of the extraordinary circumstances
  and certify the attendance. The 'I' grade is later converted into a regular
  letter grade after the student completes the missing evaluation.

## Re-evaluation / rechecking of answer sheets at DTU

Source: https://exam.dtu.ac.in/downloads1/RECHECKING.pdf

- Apply for rechecking within seven days of the declaration of the result
  (regular and ex-students of B.Tech / M.Tech (FT/PT) / MBA / B.Tech Evening).
- The rechecking fee is Rs. 200 per application; attach a photocopy of the
  admit card.
- Rechecking is NOT available for practical examinations — only theory
  courses. Rechecking verifies that all attempted questions were marked and
  totals are correct (it is not a fresh evaluation).
- Corrections in students' particulars (name, roll number, marksheets,
  duplicate documents) use a separate requisition form, also to be submitted
  within 7 days of result publication.

## Passing criteria, credits and degree requirements at DTU

Source: https://dtu.ac.in/Web/Academics/ordinance/BTech_2022_ordinance.pdf

- Passing a course requires at least 35% marks (grade P, grade point 4) and
  appearing in the End-Term Examination.
- Credits for courses with grade P or higher count as Earned Credits. A
  student who has earned the number of credits specified in the curriculum of
  his/her programme is eligible for the award of the degree.
- There is NO provision for grade improvement/betterment after passing a
  course — only courses with an 'F' grade are repeated.
- Use of unfair means in examinations attracts penalties per the ordinance's
  unfair-means annexure (result withheld, course cancellation and
  disciplinary action depending on severity, decided by the disciplinary
  authority).
- DTU also offers Minor specializations (44 minors across departments, e.g.
  Machine Learning by CSE, Data Analytics by SE, VLSI Design by ECE) earned
  by completing the prescribed basket of additional courses alongside the
  major degree.
