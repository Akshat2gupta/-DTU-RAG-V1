# DTU Hostels — Fees, Rules, Allotment, Mess (curated)

Hand-verified from the DTU Hostel Bulletin of Information 2024-25 and the
Hostel Allotment Policy (hostels.dtu.ac.in). Summarizes the rules students
most commonly ask about; the full bulletin remains the authoritative source.

## DTU hostel fee per year

Source: https://hostels.dtu.ac.in/pages/about-us/Hostel-Bulletine-2024-25.pdf

Hostel fee per year (Bulletin 2024-25), by room type:

| Component | Single seater (non-AC) | Double seater (non-AC) | Triple seater (non-AC) | Triple seater (AC) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hostel room rent | 44,000 | 40,000 | 36,000 | 36,000 |
| Electricity & water charges | 4,000 | 4,000 | 4,000 | 24,000 |
| Security & attendant services | 4,000 | 4,000 | 4,000 | 4,000 |

Mess charges and certain advances/deposits are additional (mess facility is
compulsory). For the exact current total, consult the bulletin or the hostel
office. Refund rules: Rs. 3,000 deducted if the allotted room is not taken
within a week; Rs. 6,000 deducted if vacated within a month; no refund after
staying more than a month.

## DTU hostel allotment — who gets it and for how long

Source: https://hostels.dtu.ac.in/pages/about-us/Hostel-Bulletine-2024-25.pdf

- DTU hostels are for full-time B.Tech, B.Des, M.Tech, MBA, M.Sc, M.Des and
  Ph.D students. DTU has 13 hostels — 10 for boys and 3 for girls. The
  Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam Hostel (boys) is AC, and the Virangana Lakshmibai
  Hostel (girls) has AC blocks.
- Hostel stay is permitted for the programme duration from the date of
  admission: B.Tech/B.Des 4 years, M.Tech/MBA/M.Sc/M.Design 2 years,
  Ph.D 3 years.
- For first-year B.Tech students, first preference for hostel accommodation
  goes to Outside-Delhi category students (as mentioned in the admission
  slip).
- Applications are made online via the hostel website (hostels.dtu.ac.in)
  as per the Hostel Allotment Policy for the year.
- Accommodation follows the DTU academic calendar; residents must vacate
  within one week of their last end-semester examination.

## DTU hostel rules — curfew, visitors, discipline

Source: https://hostels.dtu.ac.in/pages/about-us/Hostel-Bulletine-2024-25.pdf

- Girl hostellers must be back on campus by 9:00 PM and in their hostel by
  11:00 PM; disciplinary action follows for defaulters. Hostel functions must
  normally end by 11:00 PM.
- Residents must sign attendance regularly; failing to sign for seven
  consecutive days without the Warden's permission cancels the accommodation
  without notice.
- No hostel resident may keep a motorised vehicle in the university;
  bicycles are permitted.
- Ragging is strictly prohibited inside and outside the hostel/campus
  (Supreme Court orders apply); freshers should report incidents to the
  Warden/Chief Warden immediately.
- No female visitor is permitted in boys' hostel rooms and no male visitor in
  the girls' hostel; visitors meet residents in the visitors' room only.
- Misbehaviour with staff/security, damaging property, or violating
  discipline can lead to fines, eviction or expulsion from the hostel; the
  Warden Council's decision is final. Non-payment of dues also cancels
  allotment.
- Day scholars and outsiders are not permitted to stay in hostels.

## DTU hostel mess

Source: https://hostels.dtu.ac.in/pages/about-us/Hostel-Bulletine-2024-25.pdf

- Mess facility is compulsory for all hostellers — taking hostel
  accommodation means availing the mess. Mess charges are payable as part of
  hostel dues and are not waived under any circumstances.
- Each hostel has a compulsory mess committee; shouting or sitting on mess
  tables is prohibited and fined.
