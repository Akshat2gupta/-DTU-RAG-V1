## JAC Delhi 2026 registration — dates, last date to apply, fees

Source: https://jacdelhi.admissions.nic.in/

The last date to apply (register online) for DTU B.Tech admission 2026 is
June 09, 2026; a final three-day registration window runs June 26-28, 2026.

- Online registration and choice filling: May 28, 2026 (10:00 AM) to
  June 09, 2026.
- A second three-day registration window: June 26 to June 28, 2026 (before
  Round 2). No further registration is allowed after this in the whole cycle,
  including before the Spot Round.
- Registration fee: Rs. 1,500 (non-refundable).
- Choice editing is possible only while registration is open — fill maximum
  choices; there is no later window.
- Defence (CW) / Kashmiri Migrant / IIIT-D bonus-point document verification:
  June 10, 2026 at APJ Complex, NSUT, Dwarka.

## DTU B.Tech eligibility criteria (JAC Delhi 2026)

Source: https://jacdelhi.admissions.nic.in/

- Qualification: passed 10+2 (Senior School Certificate) with at least 60%
  aggregate marks in Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics (PCM), passed English
  (core or elective), and passed all other subjects separately.
- Must have a JEE (Main) 2026 Paper-I rank.
- Relaxation in the 60% PCM criterion: OBC-NCL and Defence (CW) — 55%;
  SC, ST and Persons with Disabilities (PD) — 50%. No relaxation for EWS.
- No minimum or maximum age limit.
- Delhi region (85% of seats): qualifying exam passed from a school/institute
  in NCT of Delhi. Outside Delhi region (15% of seats): all others.
- Admission is purely through JAC Delhi online counselling — DTU has no
  separate management quota for B.Tech.

## DTU B.Tech fees (JAC Delhi 2026)

Source: https://jacdelhi.admissions.nic.in/

What is the fee for the first year / first semester of B.Tech at DTU?
DTU charges its B.Tech academic fee per YEAR, not per semester:

- Total fee for 1st year of B.Tech at DTU: Rs. 2,69,700.
- Seat acceptance fee paid at the time of counselling: Rs. 95,000
  (counts toward the 1st-year fee; balance Rs. 1,74,700 paid at DTU).
- Subsequent years: 2nd year Rs. 2,86,700; 3rd year Rs. 3,06,700;
  4th year Rs. 3,22,000.
- JAC registration fee: Rs. 1,500 (non-refundable).
- Withdrawal with refund of seat acceptance fee is possible only until
  July 12, 2026, 10:30 PM (JAC Delhi 2026 cycle).
- DTU offers up to 100% tuition-fee waiver for economically weaker students
  (829 students, Rs. 9.49 crore waived in the last academic year) and a
  Rs. 10,000 merit scholarship to the topper of every B.Tech branch.
- Caution: the JAC brochure's fee table covers all five participating
  institutions. Figures like "Rs. 80,900 / Rs. 1,85,300, nil balance for
  SC/ST/PD" belong to DSEU, and "Rs. 2,70,000" to NSUT — they are NOT DTU
  fees. DTU's first-year B.Tech fee is Rs. 2,69,700.

## DTU M.Tech admission — process, eligibility, GATE and Non-GATE

Source: https://dtu.ac.in/Web/Admission/brochure/2025/m.tech_Admission_Brochure.pdf

M.Tech admission at DTU is separate from JAC Delhi (which is B.Tech only).
Per the DTU M.Tech Admission Brochure (session 2025-26; the 2026-27 brochure
is published on dtu.ac.in when the cycle opens):

- DTU offers 25 M.Tech programmes (full-time and part-time) across AC, AP,
  BT, CE, CSE, IT, SE, ECE, EE, ENV and ME departments — including CSE-AI,
  Software Engineering, Data Science, VLSI (VLS), Signal Processing (SPD),
  Microwave and Optical Communication (MOC), Structural (STE), Geotechnical
  (GTE), Environmental (ENE), Thermal (THE), Production (PRD), Industrial
  Engineering and Management (IEM), Power Systems (PSY), Power Electronics
  (PES), Control & Instrumentation (C&I) and more.
- Seats: 657 full-time seats total (24-30 per programme, category-wise
  GN/OBC/EWS/SC/ST per the brochure seat matrix), plus 5 sponsored
  (supernumerary) seats per programme (125 total) and 12-13 part-time
  (supernumerary) seats per programme.
- Full-time admission is on the basis of a valid GATE score (merit list per
  department). Non-GATE candidates can apply for admission through a DTU
  merit/admission test conducted by the university. Sponsored full-time and
  3-year part-time options exist for employed candidates (part-time requires
  the employer to be in the NCR region).
- Eligibility: qualifying degree (B.Tech/B.E. or equivalent per programme)
  with at least 60% marks or equivalent CGPA for General category
  (conversion when no formula is provided: Percentage = 10 x CGPA);
  relaxation for SC/ST/PwD/OBC categories.
- Application fee: Rs. 1,500 (all categories, online).
- Financial support: full-time GATE-qualified students can receive a
  Teaching Assistantship (DTU-TA) stipend of Rs. 7,500 per month based on
  merit and departmental requirements.
- Indicative schedule (2025 cycle): advertisement mid-May; admission test for
  Non-GATE around mid-July; last date of admission July 04 (GATE candidates)
  / July 18 (Non-GATE). Dates for each new cycle are notified on dtu.ac.in.

Source: https://dtu.ac.in/Web/Admission/brochure/2025/mtech_Provisional_Merit_List_FT_GATE.pdf

official M.Tech GATE-score cutoffs. The following indicative levels are
derived from the official Round-1 provisional merit list for Full-Time GATE
candidates, M.Tech admission 2025-26 (score of the candidate at roughly the
seat-count rank in each department's merit list):

| Department (M.Tech programmes) | GATE applicants | Top GATE score | Indicative competitive level |
|---|---|---|---|
| CSE / IT / SE combined (CSE, AI, Data Science, Software Engg, Information Systems) | 723 | 781 | ~540-615 (rank 25: 614, rank 60: 581, rank 120: 542) |
| Electronics & Communication (VLSI, SPD, MOC) | 167 | 612 | ~365-470 (rank 25: 467, rank 120: 365) |
| Biotechnology | 38 | 630 | ~300-380 — applicants ≈ seats |
| Civil Engineering (STE, GTE, HFE, GINF) | 34 | 528 | ~250-330 — applicants ≈ seats |
| Electrical Engineering (PSY, PES, C&I) | 26 | 531 | ~255 — fewer applicants than seats |
| Mechanical Engineering (THE, PRD, IEM, CAAD) | 21 | 495 | ~280 — fewer applicants than seats |
| Environmental / Applied Physics | very few | 450 / 241 | effectively any valid GATE score |

Key takeaways: the computing programmes (CSE/AI/DS/SE/IT) are by far the most
competitive — realistically a GATE score around 550-620+ was needed in 2025.
ECE needed roughly 370-470. For most core branches (EE, ME, CE, BT, ENE, AP)
the number of GATE applicants was close to or below the seat count, so any
valid GATE score with eligibility had a strong chance, and remaining seats go
to Non-GATE candidates through the DTU admission test. Final admission varies
by round, category and dropouts — verify against the department-wise
admission lists on dtu.ac.in.

## How DTU admission works — quick summary

Source: https://jacdelhi.admissions.nic.in/

1. Appear in JEE (Main) 2026 Paper-I.
2. Register online at jacdelhi.admissions.nic.in (May 28 - June 09, 2026,
   Rs. 1,500) and fill branch/institute preferences for the five participating
   institutions (DTU, NSUT, IIIT-Delhi, IGDTUW, DSEU). 7,765 engineering seats
   total across the five institutions; about 2,750 at DTU.
3. Seats are allotted over 4 rounds + upgradation + spot round based on JEE
   rank, category and region (June 15 - July 21, 2026).
4. When allotted a seat: pay Rs. 95,000 seat acceptance fee online and report
   to DTU campus for document verification on your category's scheduled day.
5. Freeze the seat to stop upgradation, or stay in for automatic upgradation
   to higher preferences in later rounds.
