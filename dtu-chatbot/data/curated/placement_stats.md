## M.Tech branches and specializations offered at DTU

Source: https://tnp.dtu.ac.in/docs/stats/2425.pdf

What branches does M.Tech have at DTU? Beyond the branches on the DTU academics
page, the M.Tech specializations participating in DTU placements in 2024-25 were:
Computer Science and Engineering (CSE), Software Engineering (SWE),
Information Systems (ISY), Data Science (DSC), Artificial Intelligence (AFI —
the M.Tech AI program), Power Electronics and Systems (PES), Control and
Instrumentation (C&I), Industrial Engineering and Management (IEM), Structural
Engineering (STE), Bioinformatics (BIO), Signal Processing and Digital Design
(SPD), VLSI Design and Embedded Systems (VLS), Computational Design (CAD), and
Geotechnical Engineering (GTE). Earlier years also include Power Systems (PSY),
Thermal Engineering (THE), Production Engineering (PRD), Hydraulics and Flood
Engineering (HFE), Geoinformatics (GEO), Environmental Engineering (ENE),
Industrial Biotechnology (IBT), and Microwave and Optical Communication (MOC).

Note: M.Tech in Artificial Intelligence and M.Tech in Data Science are offered
by the CSE department; some older DTU academics pages list M.Tech branches
without these newer programs.

## Top recruiters — which companies come to DTU for placements

Source: https://dtu.ac.in/Web/Academics/ordinance/BTech_2025_ordinance.pdf

Top recruiters at DTU include Google, Amazon, Microsoft, Uber, Sprinklr,
Uniqlo, Goldman Sachs, Qualcomm, Texas Instruments, Synopsys, SanDisk,
Nvidia, Maruti, Samsung, Bain, KPMG, McKinsey, PwC, GAIL, BEL and C-DOT.
Earlier years also saw Facebook (Meta), Bank of America, JP Morgan,
Tata Steel, Tata Motors, L&T and major PSUs (Indian Navy, Indian Air Force,
NTPC, BPCL, IOCL).

Record packages cited in DTU's own documents: the highest international
salary reached 1.27 crore LPA (Uber) and the highest domestic salary 38 LPA
(Adobe); the T&P statistics sheets record a 184 LPA (1.84 crore) maximum CTC
for COE in 2021-22. Leading companies also offer paid internships to
third-year students, which often convert to pre-placement offers (PPOs).

## Overall summary across years

- Overall B.Tech placement rate (placed / registered): about 66% (2019-20),
  68% (2020-21), 77% (2021-22), 69% (2022-23). The 2023-24 and 2024-25 sheets
  publish offers rather than placed counts, so a placement rate cannot be
  computed from them; IT department NAAC data shows 67.5% (2023-24) and 64.6%
  (2024-25, partial year).
- Highest package ever recorded in these sheets: 184 LPA (1.84 crore), COE,
  2021-22 batch. Recent years: 85.30 LPA (2023-24), 85 LPA (2024-25, SE).
- Highest branch average CTC: COE/IT/SE consistently lead — 23.7-25.2 LPA
  (2022-23), 17-21.5 LPA (2023-24), 16.6-19.9 LPA (2024-25).
- Circuit branches (COE, IT, SE, MCE, ECE) place noticeably better than core
  branches (CE, ENE, BT) in every year.
- M.Tech Artificial Intelligence (AI / AFI) across years — 2022-23: 21 placed /
  25 registered, average 19.58 LPA, maximum 51.03 LPA; 2023-24: 22 offers /
  28 registered, average 23.04 LPA; 2024-25: 23 offers / 30 registered,
  average 14.02 LPA, maximum 28 LPA.
